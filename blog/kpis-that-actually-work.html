KPIs That Actually Work: A Framework for Indian Organisations

RV
Rohit Verma
Head of Performance & Rewards
May 2026 · 7 min read

Ask most employees what their KPIs are, and you'll hear things like "submit reports on time," "attend training sessions," or "respond to emails within 24 hours." These are activities. They are not performance.

Effective KPIs answer one question: did this person's work move the business forward? Everything else is noise.

If your KPIs can be achieved by someone who is failing, they are not KPIs. They are tasks.

The KRA-KPI Distinction

A Key Result Area (KRA) defines the domain of responsibility — the area where someone is accountable for creating value. A Key Performance Indicator (KPI) measures performance within that area. Most organisations skip the KRA step and jump straight to metrics — which is why their KPIs end up measuring the wrong things.

The Five Tests of a Good KPI

  • Outcome, not output — measures what changed, not what was done
  • Controllable — the employee has genuine influence over the result
  • Measurable — can be quantified with available data, not estimated subjectively
  • Aligned — connects directly to a business goal at team or company level
  • Time-bound — has a clear measurement period and target

Common KPI Mistakes We See

The most common KPI design mistakes we see in Indian organisations: using the same generic KPIs for everyone regardless of role, setting targets without involving the employee in the process, measuring too many things (more than 5–7 KPIs per person creates paralysis), and never revising KPIs when business priorities change mid-year.

Connecting KPIs to Rewards

KPIs only motivate when they're connected to meaningful consequences — positive and negative. This doesn't always mean variable pay. Recognition, career progression, autonomy, and development opportunities are powerful motivators when tied clearly to performance outcomes. The key is consistency: if high performance is rewarded unpredictably or inequitably, KPIs stop being motivating very quickly.

Getting Started

Start with your top three business goals for the year. For each goal, identify the two or three roles that have the most influence over it. Design KPIs for those roles first. Then cascade downward. You'll find that the act of doing this reveals misalignments in your org structure that are worth fixing.

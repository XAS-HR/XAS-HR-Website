Do You Need a Fractional CHRO? A Practical Guide for Growing Businesses

DK
Divya Krishnan
HR Strategy & Advisory Lead
March 2026 · 8 min read

At some point in every growing organisation, the founder or CEO realises that HR is no longer something they can manage with a generalist and good intentions. The people problems are becoming strategic — culture drift as the team scales, leadership gaps, talent pipeline concerns, a compliance landscape that's grown more complex than anyone expected.

The instinct is to hire a CHRO. But a good CHRO at the senior level commands ₹40–80 lakhs per year in India. For a 100–300 person organisation still investing heavily in growth, that's a significant fixed cost commitment. And if the hire is wrong — which at CHRO level happens more often than people admit — the cost of unwinding it is substantial.

The question isn't whether you need strategic HR leadership. It's whether you need it full-time, right now.

The Fractional CHRO Model

A Fractional CHRO is an experienced senior HR leader who works with your organisation on a retained, part-time basis — typically 8–15 days per month. They attend your leadership team meetings, own the people agenda, build and manage the HR function, and bring the strategic capability of a full-time CHRO at roughly 30–40% of the cost.

When Does It Make Sense?

  • You're between 50 and 500 employees and HR is becoming genuinely strategic
  • You have an HR manager or team but no senior HR leadership
  • You're preparing for a fundraising round or acquisition where people due diligence will matter
  • You're scaling rapidly and culture is at risk
  • You have a specific HR transformation to drive — HRIS implementation, compliance overhaul, org restructure
  • Your CEO is spending more than 20% of their time on people issues

What a Fractional CHRO Is Not

A fractional CHRO is not a consultant who delivers a report and leaves. The model works because of continuity — the fractional leader becomes part of your leadership team, builds institutional knowledge, and drives the people agenda over a sustained period (typically 12–24 months). Think of it as renting a CHRO, not hiring a consultant.

When to Move to a Full-Time CHRO

The transition to a full-time CHRO typically makes sense when the organisation has 500+ employees, the HR function has grown to 5+ people, or the complexity of the people agenda requires daily senior HR presence. A well-structured fractional arrangement often prepares the ground for this transition — building the function, establishing the processes, and sometimes even identifying and onboarding the permanent hire.

Making the Decision

Start by answering three questions: Is HR currently a competitive advantage or a liability for your organisation? Are you making major people decisions without adequate strategic input? Could HR be materially better with stronger leadership? If the answer to any of these is yes, the fractional model is worth a conversation.

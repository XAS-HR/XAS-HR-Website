Why HR Compliance Is a Business Risk, Not Just an HR Problem

PS
Priya Sharma
Head of HR Compliance
June 2026 · 6 min read

Most founders and CEOs think of HR compliance as paperwork — something the HR team handles quietly in the background. That assumption is expensive.

In 2025, the number of labour tribunal filings in India increased by 18% year-on-year. The average settlement cost for a wrongful termination claim: ₹8.2 lakhs. The average time lost by leadership dealing with a POSH complaint that wasn't properly managed: 4–6 months of distraction.

HR compliance isn't HR's problem. It's the CEO's problem that HR is there to prevent.

The Most Common Compliance Gaps We See

After auditing HR compliance at over 100 Indian organisations, these are the gaps that appear most frequently:

  • Employment contracts that don't include non-compete or IP assignment clauses
  • POSH Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) not constituted — or constituted incorrectly
  • PF and ESI contributions calculated on base salary only, excluding allowances that should be included
  • No documented disciplinary procedure — making terminations legally vulnerable
  • Shops & Establishments Act registration lapsed or in the wrong state
  • Contract workers engaged without CLRA licence — a criminal liability

The Cost of Getting It Wrong

Each of these gaps carries financial, operational, and reputational risk. PF on under-declared wages can result in back contributions plus 12% interest plus penalty. POSH non-compliance carries personal criminal liability for directors. A botched termination can result in reinstatement orders or substantial compensation awards.

What a Compliance Audit Covers

A structured HR compliance audit reviews your organisation across five dimensions: statutory registrations, employment documentation, payroll and benefits compliance, workplace safety and POSH, and contract workforce management. The output is a prioritised action plan — not a list of problems, but a sequenced roadmap for resolving them.

Most organisations complete a compliance clean-up within 60–90 days. The peace of mind is immediate; the legal protection is permanent.

Where to Start

If you haven't reviewed your HR compliance position in the last 12 months, start with a self-assessment against the six gaps listed above. If any of them apply to your organisation, it's worth a structured audit before you're audited externally.
